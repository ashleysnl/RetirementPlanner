What is OAS clawback?

OAS clawback is a recovery tax applied when your taxable income is above the annual threshold. The higher your taxable income, the more OAS can be reduced.

In retirement, large RRSP/RRIF withdrawals can increase taxable income and push you into clawback territory.

How is clawback estimated here?

This simulator uses planning-level tax and clawback tables. It is not tax advice.

Can delaying CPP/OAS reduce clawback?

It can help or hurt depending on your withdrawal pattern and taxable income in each year. Use timing simulator comparisons in the main calculator.

Does this include RRIF minimums?

Yes, when RRIF rules are enabled, minimum withdrawals are modeled and can influence clawback risk later in retirement.
