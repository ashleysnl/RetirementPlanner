How RRIF minimums change retirement income

Once RRSP assets convert to RRIF, annual minimum withdrawals apply and generally increase with age. This can force taxable income higher than your spending need.

That is why gross withdrawals can be larger than your net spending gap and why tax drag can rise later in retirement.

Do RRIF minimums increase every year?

The required percentage generally rises with age, which can increase taxable withdrawals over time.

Can RRIF minimums increase OAS clawback?

Yes. Higher taxable income from RRIF withdrawals can raise clawback exposure in some years.

Is this financial advice?

No. This is a planning-level estimate to help you understand retirement tradeoffs.
